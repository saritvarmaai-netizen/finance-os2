## INCOME TAX SLABS — FY 2025-26

### New Regime (Section 115BAC — Default)
| Income Range | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹3,00,000 | Nil |
| ₹3,00,001 – ₹7,00,000 | 5% |
| ₹7,00,001 – ₹10,00,000 | 10% |
| ₹10,00,001 – ₹12,00,000 | 15% |
| ₹12,00,001 – ₹15,00,000 | 20% |
| Above ₹15,00,000 | 30% |

New Regime Deductions ALLOWED:
- Standard deduction: ₹75,000 (salaried)
- NPS employer contribution: 80CCD(2)
- NPS own contribution: 80CCD(1B) up to ₹50,000
- Rebate u/s 87A: Tax nil if income ≤ ₹7,00,000

New Regime Deductions NOT ALLOWED:
- 80C (LIC, PPF, ELSS, EPF, home loan principal): Not allowed
- 80D (health insurance): Not allowed
- HRA exemption: Not allowed
- LTA: Not allowed
- Interest on housing loan (self-occupied): Not allowed

### Old Regime
| Income Range | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹2,50,000 | Nil |
| ₹2,50,001 – ₹5,00,000 | 5% |
| ₹5,00,001 – ₹10,00,000 | 20% |
| Above ₹10,00,000 | 30% |

Old Regime Key Deductions:
- Standard deduction: ₹50,000 (salaried)
- 80C: Up to ₹1,50,000 (LIC, PPF, ELSS, 5-yr FD, EPF, home loan principal, children tuition)
- 80CCD(1B): NPS additional contribution ₹50,000 (over and above 80C)
- 80D: ₹25,000 self + family; ₹50,000 if parents are senior citizens
- 80TTA: Interest on savings account up to ₹10,000
- HRA: Exempt based on actual rent, 40%/50% of salary, actual HRA — whichever is least
- Rebate u/s 87A: Tax nil if income ≤ ₹5,00,000

### Surcharge (both regimes)
- Income 50L–1Cr: 10% surcharge on tax
- Income 1Cr–2Cr: 15% surcharge
- Income above 2Cr: 25% surcharge (marginal relief applies)

### Health & Education Cess
- 4% on (tax + surcharge) — applies to all taxpayers

### Breakeven Formula
Old regime wins when: Total deductions > (Breakeven deduction level for your income)
For ₹15-18L income: Need deductions > ~₹3.5-4L for old regime to win
For ₹10-15L income: Need deductions > ~₹2.5-3L for old regime to win

## CAPITAL GAINS TAXATION

### Equity Mutual Funds & Direct Equity Shares

**Long Term Capital Gains (LTCG)**
- Holding period: MORE than 12 months from date of purchase
- Rate: 12.5% on gains above ₹1,25,000 per year
- Exemption: ₹1,25,000 per financial year (increased from ₹1L in Budget 2024)
- STT must have been paid on original purchase
- Grandfathering: Cost for pre-Jan 31, 2018 holdings = Jan 31, 2018 price or actual, whichever higher

**Short Term Capital Gains (STCG)**
- Holding period: 12 months or less
- Rate: 20% (increased from 15% in Budget 2024)
- No exemption limit. First rupee is taxable.

**Key Tax Harvesting Rule**
LTCG harvesting = selling units to "book" gain below ₹1,25,000, then repurchasing.
The new cost basis resets to the higher price, reducing future LTCG.
LTCG clock resets on repurchase — must hold new units 12+ months for LTCG again.
No STT issue if same fund, different folio or same folio different date.

### Debt Mutual Funds (after April 1, 2023)
- All holding periods: Taxed as per income slab (no LTCG/STCG distinction)
- No indexation benefit for funds purchased after April 1, 2023
- Effective rate: 30% for highest slab taxpayers

### Fixed Deposit Interest
- Taxed as income in the year of accrual (not receipt)
- TDS deducted at 10% if interest > ₹40,000 p.a. per bank (₹50,000 for senior citizens)
- If no PAN: TDS at 20%
- Form 15G/H: Submit to avoid TDS if total income below exemption limit

### Dividend Income
- Fully taxable as income in hands of investor (DDT abolished from FY 2020-21)
- TDS at 10% if dividend > ₹5,000 per company per year
- No special rate — added to income and taxed at slab rate

## ADVANCE TAX SYSTEM

### Who Must Pay
Advance tax applies if estimated tax liability for the year > ₹10,000 after TDS.

### Due Dates and Percentages
| Installment | Due Date | Cumulative % of Annual Tax |
|---|---|---|
| Q1 | 15 June | 15% |
| Q2 | 15 September | 45% |
| Q3 | 15 December | 75% |
| Q4 | 15 March | 100% |

### Penalty for Non-Payment / Underpayment
- Section 234B: 1% per month if advance tax paid < 90% of total tax
- Section 234C: 1% per month on shortfall for each installment
- These are simple interest calculations, not compound

### Calculation Approach
1. Estimate total income for FY from all sources (salary + FD interest + dividends + capital gains)
2. Calculate total tax liability
3. Deduct TDS already deducted
4. Balance is advance tax payable in installments

## HUF — HINDU UNDIVIDED FAMILY

### What is HUF
- Separate legal entity for tax purposes — has its own PAN
- Files separate ITR each year
- Income earned by HUF is taxed in HUF's hands, not individual's
- Can hold property, bank accounts, make investments

### HUF Tax Rules
- Same tax slabs as individual (both new and old regime applicable)
- HUF can also claim standard deduction, 80C, 80D etc.
- HUF can have its own FDs, MF investments, real estate

### What Can Be HUF Income
- Interest from HUF FDs
- Rental income from HUF property
- Business income of HUF
- Capital gains on HUF investments

### What CANNOT Be HUF Income
- Salary of any member (always taxed in individual's hands)
- Professional income of any member

### Strategy
HUF is powerful when: Individual is in 30% slab but HUF income falls in 0-5% slab
Example: ₹4L HUF FD interest = taxed at 5% in HUF vs 30% in individual = save ~₹10,000/year

## CLUBBING OF INCOME — MINOR CHILD

### Rule
All income (interest, dividends, capital gains) earned by a minor child (under 18)
is clubbed with the income of the parent with higher income.

### Application to This Household
IDFC Child Savings account and its FD: All interest earned here is added to Self's income
for tax calculation. The child's name on the account does not change this.

### Exemption
Up to ₹1,500 per child per year is exempt from clubbing (negligible exemption)

### Planning Note
When the child turns 18, this clubbing stops and they file their own ITR.
